Sky's All-Time Film Favorites

Interstellar (2014)

In a future earth facing blight and famine, a group of astronauts travel through a wormhole near Saturn to search for a new home for humankind.

Cast: Matthew McConaughey, Anne Hathaway, Jessica Chastain, Michael Caine
Reviews: Paramount to an act of God. Delivers a powerful message with great scientific accuracy. Hans Zimmer's music is phenomenal beyond comprehension. (Personal)
This is not only a tremendously exciting space adventure... It's also a rich and thoughtful meditation on time and space and gravity. (David Stratton, At The Movies Australia)
FINAL SCORE

5.0

Munich - The Edge of War (2021)

In the fall of 1938, a British civil servant and a German diplomat, former friends, travel to Munich to prevent war as Hitler prepares to invade Czechoslovakia.

Cast: George MacKay, Jannis Niewohner, Jeremy Irons, Sandra Huller
Reviews: Messy and dark at times, the film delivers a poignant message on war and peace, Neville Chamberlain's policy of appeasement, and the ramifications of small decisions on the world at large. (Personal)
If the movie is handsome in an oak-paneled-office way, there's life in it too. You feel there's something at stake for the two young would-be heroes, as there is for the world. (Stephanie Zacharek, TIME Magazine)
FINAL SCORE

4.0

Your Name (Kimi no Nawa) (2016)

Two teenagers share a magical connection after they discover they are swapping bodies. The situation complicates as both attempt to meet - crossing time and fate.

Cast: Ryunosuke Kamiki, Mone Kamishiraishi
Reviews: A well-made, well-produced, eye-waterning movie that focuses on themes like love, loss and family. (Personal)
The only way to understand it is to swim in it for yourself, feel your own heart braid around these two interwoven lives, and gaze up in awe at the silvery arc those falling stars trace across the sky. (Robbie Collin, The Telegraph)
FINAL SCORE

5.0

Midway (2019)

The U.S. Navy and a squad of brave fighter pilots engage the Japanese enemy at Midway directly following the December 7th attacks on Pearl Harbor.

Cast: Patrick Wilson, Aaron Eckhart, Nick Jonas, Mandy Moore, Dennis Quaid, Woody Harrelson
Reviews: A shockingly accurate, powerful, brutal film from Roland Emmerich. Displays well the personal relationships and the costs of war while portraying the side of conflict not often shown; that of the Navy. (Personal)
"Midway" tells a story that's vividly and viscerally rendered, with all the entertainment value of a big, old-fashioned war movie, cutting back and forth between the home front and front line. (Michael O'Sullivan, Washington Post)
FINAL SCORE

4.0
